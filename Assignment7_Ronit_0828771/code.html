The marine environment spans approximately 71% of the Earth's surface and is home to a vast array of life forms, ranging from tiny phytoplankton to immense blue whales. These oceans are crucial for regulating the planet's climate, absorbing carbon dioxide, and providing essential resources and food for billions of people.

The marine environment is distinguished by its unique physical and chemical properties, including high salinity, pressure, and variable temperatures. These factors, combined with dynamic currents, tides, and waves, create challenging habitats for marine life, leading to a wide range of specialized adaptations among organisms.

Human activities have had a significant impact on marine ecosystems, resulting in very harmful issues. These threats pose serious risks to marine biodiversity and ecosystem health, highlighting the urgent need for conservation efforts to protect and sustainably manage marine resources for future generations.
